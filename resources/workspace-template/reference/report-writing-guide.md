# NZ School Report Writing Guide

A guide to writing effective student report comments in the NZ school context. Use this as a reference when using the `/report-comments` command or writing reports manually.

---

## Principles of Good NZ School Reports

NZ schools are guided by the idea that reports should:

1. **Be meaningful to parents and whānau** — written in plain language, not educational jargon
2. **Be strengths-based** — lead with what the student can do
3. **Be specific** — reference actual skills, behaviours, or learning rather than generic praise
4. **Include next steps** — give families something to look forward to or work on together
5. **Be honest** — even when achievement is below expectations, the tone is supportive and growth-oriented
6. **Reflect the whole child** — where appropriate, reference wellbeing, attitude, and character alongside academic achievement

---

## Comment Structure

A well-structured report comment typically follows this pattern:

> **[Strength]** + **[Evidence or specifics]** + **[Achievement level relative to curriculum]** + **[Next step or growth area]**

**Example (Year 5 Reading, At Level):**
> *"[Name] reads with fluency and expression, showing strong comprehension of a range of texts. [He/She/They] is meeting curriculum expectations and consistently makes connections between texts and [his/her/their] own experiences. The next step is to develop skills in inferring meaning from more complex texts — something [Name] is already beginning to explore."*

---

## Achievement Level Language

Match your language to where the student is working relative to the curriculum:

| Level | Suggested Opening |
| ----- | ----------------- |
| Well Below | "[Name] is working toward curriculum expectations in [subject]..." |
| Below | "[Name] is developing [his/her/their] skills in [subject] and is working toward Level X..." |
| At | "[Name] is meeting curriculum expectations in [subject] at Level X..." |
| Above | "[Name] is achieving beyond curriculum expectations, working confidently at Level X..." |

For NCEA: reference the standard name, grade (N/A/M/E), and what the student demonstrated.

---

## Language to Use

**Positive, active verbs:**
- demonstrates, applies, explores, investigates, creates, communicates, analyses, evaluates, connects, constructs, contributes, reflects

**Phrases for next steps (positive framing):**
- "The next step is..."
- "We are working toward..."
- "Continuing to develop..."
- "An exciting next challenge is..."
- "[Name] is beginning to explore..."

---

## Language to Avoid

| Avoid | Better alternative |
| ----- | ------------------ |
| "Can't do..." | "Is working toward..." |
| "Struggles with..." | "Is developing skills in..." |
| "Lazy" / "unmotivated" | "When engaged, [Name] shows..." / "With support..." |
| "Below average" | "Below curriculum expectations" |
| "Good effort" (vague) | Specify what the effort looks like |
| Educational jargon (e.g., "phonemic awareness") | "Reading the sounds in words" |

## Subject-Specific Tips

### Reading
- Reference the type of texts the student reads (fiction, non-fiction, digital)
- Mention skills: fluency, comprehension, inference, vocabulary, making connections
- Note reading for enjoyment/attitude where relevant

### Writing
- Reference the types of writing (narrative, explanatory, persuasive, procedural)
- Mention: structure, ideas, vocabulary, spelling, punctuation
- Acknowledge the writing process (planning, drafting, editing)

### Mathematics
- Be strand-specific (Number, Algebra, Geometry, Statistics) if space allows
- Reference real-world application of maths where possible
- Note reasoning and problem-solving, not just computation

### Science
- Connect to Nature of Science (asking questions, investigating, communicating findings)
- Reference specific scientific content from the unit

### Social Sciences
- Note the student's ability to make connections to the world around them
- Reference inquiry skills, empathy, perspective-taking

---

## Practical Tips

- **Word count**: Most NZ schools allow 2–5 sentences per subject. Check your school's expectation.
- **Personalisation**: Always insert the student's name and use correct pronouns. Generic comments feel impersonal.
- **Voice**: Write as though speaking directly to the parent/caregiver, with warmth.
- **Consistency**: Maintain a consistent tone across comments for the same class — parents compare notes.
- **Proofreading**: Check for typos, correct pronouns, and that the comment matches the grade awarded.

## Sample Comments by Level

### Reading — Year 3, Below Level
> "[Name] is developing [his/her/their] reading skills and working toward curriculum Level 2. [He/She/They] reads familiar texts with growing confidence and is beginning to apply decoding strategies for new words. Our next focus is building fluency with a wider range of texts — something we are working on together in class each day."

### Mathematics — Year 6, At Level
> "[Name] demonstrates solid understanding of number and algebra at curriculum Level 3. [He/She/They] applies multiplication and division strategies accurately and is beginning to explore number patterns with confidence. The next step is to extend this thinking into more complex problem-solving situations."

### Writing — Year 10, Above Level
> "[Name] writes with clarity, style, and a strong sense of audience at a Level 5 standard. [His/Her/Their] persuasive writing in particular demonstrates sophisticated use of language and well-structured arguments. We look forward to seeing [Name] continue to refine [his/her/their] writing for different purposes and contexts."

### Science — NCEA Level 1, Merit
> "[Name] achieved Merit in AS91159 (Demonstrate understanding of aspects of genetics). [He/She/They] showed a clear understanding of inheritance patterns and was able to apply this knowledge to explain genetic variation in a range of contexts. Well done."
