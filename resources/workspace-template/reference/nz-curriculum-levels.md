# NZ Curriculum Levels — Quick Reference

This reference maps NZ Curriculum levels to school years and provides a guide to the achievement objective structure across learning areas. Use this when writing lesson plans, unit plans, or report comments.

---

## Curriculum Level to Year Level Mapping

| Curriculum Level | Approximate School Years |
| ---------------- | ------------------------ |
| Level 1          | Years 1–2                |
| Level 2          | Years 3–4                |
| Level 3          | Years 5–6                |
| Level 4          | Years 7–8                |
| Level 5          | Years 9–10               |
| Level 6          | Year 11 (NCEA Level 1)   |
| Level 7          | Year 12 (NCEA Level 2)   |
| Level 8          | Year 13 (NCEA Level 3)   |

> Note: These are approximate — students progress at different rates, and some schools use different descriptors.

## Key Competencies

All lesson and unit plans should identify which Key Competencies are developed:

1. **Thinking** — Using creative, critical, metacognitive, and reflective processes to make sense of information, experiences, and ideas
2. **Using language, symbols, and texts** — Working with and making meaning of the codes in which knowledge is expressed (language, numbers, images, ICT, etc.)
3. **Managing self** — Being self-motivated, reliable, resilient; setting personal goals and managing learning
4. **Relating to others** — Interacting effectively with a diverse range of people; listening actively, recognising different points of view
5. **Participating and contributing** — Contributing to communities as a critical, informed, and responsible citizen

## Learning Areas Overview

### English
- **Strands**: Listening, Reading, and Viewing; Speaking, Writing, and Presenting
- **Progressions run from Level 1–8**
- At primary level, reference the Literacy Learning Progressions alongside NZC

### Mathematics and Statistics
- **Strands**: Number and Algebra; Geometry and Measurement; Statistics
- Progress from Level 1 (counting, basic shapes) to Level 8 (calculus, probability)

### Science
- **Strands**: Nature of Science (overarching); Living World; Planet Earth and Beyond; Physical World; Material World
- Nature of Science is the integrating strand — always include it

### Social Sciences
- **Strands**: Identity, Culture, and Organisation; Place and Environment; Continuity and Change; The Economic World
- Strong links to local history, community, and identity — ideal for cultural responsiveness

### Technology
- **Strands**: Technological Practice; Technological Knowledge; Nature of Technology
- Includes digital technologies (Computational Thinking, Designing and Developing Digital Outcomes)

### The Arts
- **Disciplines**: Dance, Drama, Music, Visual Arts
- Each has its own strand structure at each level

### Health and Physical Education
- **Strands**: Personal Health and Physical Development; Movement Concepts and Motor Skills; Relationships with Other People; Healthy Communities and Environments

### Languages (including Te Reo Māori)
- Separate curriculum documents apply
- Te Reo Māori has its own curriculum: *Te Marautanga o Aotearoa* for immersion; NZC for second-language contexts

## NCEA (Years 11–13)

- **NCEA Level 1** ≈ Year 11 (NZC Level 6)
- **NCEA Level 2** ≈ Year 12 (NZC Level 7)
- **NCEA Level 3** ≈ Year 13 (NZC Level 8)

Achievement standards are assessed as:
- **N** — Not Achieved
- **A** — Achieved
- **M** — Merit
- **E** — Excellence

## Reporting Language Aligned to Curriculum

| NZC Descriptor | Common Report Phrasing |
| -------------- | ---------------------- |
| Well Below curriculum level | "Working toward curriculum expectations" |
| Below curriculum level | "Developing skills at curriculum Level X" |
| At curriculum level | "Meeting curriculum expectations at Level X" |
| Above curriculum level | "Achieving beyond curriculum expectations at Level X" |
